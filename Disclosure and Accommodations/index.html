7.2 The Decision

Should you disclose?

Answer the questions below honestly. The tool will help you think through where you stand — not tell you what to do. This decision is yours.

⚖️ Work through the decision

1 Are you currently struggling with something in your work environment that an accommodation could address?
2 Do you have a sense of what specific accommodation you'd request?
3 How would you describe trust with your manager right now?
4 Based on what you've seen, how does your workplace handle disability-related matters?
5 Are you currently in a performance conversation or under scrutiny at work?
6 Do you currently have a healthcare provider who could write documentation if requested?
This tool doesn't decide for you. It helps you see your answers in one place. The right decision depends on factors only you have full visibility into. Use this as a thinking prompt, not a verdict.

Flexible start or end times

Additional time for work products that require sustained attention.
Attendance modifications for medical appointments
Flexible attendance to accommodate treatment schedules.
Protected focus time blocks (no meetings)
Reserved time each day for deep or complex work.
💬 Communication and Information ▶
Written instructions in addition to verbal ones
Reduces reliance on working memory during task handoffs.
Written meeting agendas distributed in advance
Supports preparation and reduces anxiety about unexpected topics.
Recorded meetings or written summaries afterward
Supports processing and retention for meetings with dense information.
Email-first communication preference
Written channel as primary mode rather than in-person or phone.
Written feedback in addition to verbal performance reviews
Supports processing of feedback and reduces in-the-moment overwhelm.
Advance notice for non-routine requests or changes
Reduces difficulty with unexpected transitions and last-minute demands.
🧩 How Work Is Structured ▶
Remote or hybrid work arrangement
Ability to work from home some or all of the time to manage environment.
Reduced or modified attendance at non-essential meetings
Limits social and cognitive load from meetings that don't require your input.
Task management support or structured check-ins
Regular structured touchpoints to support prioritization and executive function.
Job restructuring (redistribution of non-essential functions)
Shifting tasks that are not central to your role to other team members.
Remember: You don't have to request everything at once. Start with the accommodation that would make the biggest practical difference right now.

7.6 Early Warning Check

Recognize the need before burnout

Answer these questions about how work has felt over the past few weeks. This is for your awareness only — nothing is submitted or saved beyond this browser session.

🔍 How often do you experience this?

I feel more exhausted after work than the effort I put in seems to justify.
I have difficulty switching off or recovering after the workday ends.
I feel a sense of dread about specific work situations that used to feel manageable.
I'm managing or compensating for a work difficulty rather than solving it.
I'm adjusting how I spend my personal time in order to cope with how work is affecting me.
I notice my sensory sensitivities or emotional regulation are harder outside of work hours — particularly on work days.

📝 Reflect: what might help?

If you identified one pattern above, write one thing that could change it. Not a complete solution — just one thing. That's your starting point for an accommodation conversation.
The best time to request an accommodation is before the crisis. If two or more of these patterns showed up as "often" or "almost always," that's worth paying attention to now — not after a performance conversation forces your hand.
