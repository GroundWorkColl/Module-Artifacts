3.4 Mock Interview Practice

Practice with STAR

Pick a question and draft your answer using the Situation, Task, Action, Result structure. This is a scratchpad — no one sees this but you.

STAR is a structure, not a script. Use it to keep your answers focused and complete. Practice enough that the structure becomes instinct.
"Tell me about yourself." +
S Situation
Who are you professionally? Where are you coming from?
T Task / Focus
What are you focused on now? What are you looking for?
A Action / Strengths
What do you bring? What's your strongest asset?
R Result / Why This Role
Connect your story to this specific opportunity.
"Tell me about a time you handled a challenge." +
S Situation
What was the context? What was happening?
T Task
What was your specific responsibility in that situation?
A Action
What did YOU specifically do? Be concrete about your choices.
R Result
What happened? What did you learn?
"Tell me about a time you worked with a difficult team member." +
S Situation
Brief context. What was the project or team dynamic?
T Task
What outcome was needed despite the friction?
A Action
What specific steps did you take to address it?
R Result
What happened? How did things resolve?
"What's an area you're working to improve?" +
S Situation / Honest Context
Name something real. Interviewers can tell when this is rehearsed.
T Task / Why It Matters
Why does improving this matter to you professionally?
A Action / What You're Doing
What concrete steps are you taking to improve?
R Result / Progress
What improvement have you seen, even if it's ongoing?
Feedback checklist for mock interviews: After your practice run, ask: Did my answer address what was asked? Did I go too long? Was there a moment where I lost you? What one thing should I say differently?

📌 Quick reminders

Ask for accommodations before the interview if you need them. You don't have to disclose a diagnosis.
Have your question list written down and bring it. Don't rely on memory.
Arrive 10–15 minutes early. Greet the front desk warmly. They often share impressions.
Practice out loud, not just in your head. Your brain will skip things your mouth stumbles over.
